Turtle Categories

Embark on a comprehensive exploration of the diverse range of turtle species in Sri Lanka, Taking your understanding to new depths

Green Turtle

Description

The intriguing sea animal known as the green turtle is distinguished by its vivid green coloring and substantial size.
It is prevalent in oceans all around the world, including Sri Lanka, and is essential to supporting seagrass habitats.
Conservation efforts are concentrated on preserving nesting beaches and developing sustainable fishing methods for this species,
which is endangered due to habitat degradation and poaching. By supporting
programs to protect the green turtle, we can ensure that it will live to be admired and appreciated by future generations.




Olive Ridley Turtle

Description

A type of marine turtle called the Olive Ridley turtle is well-known for its distinctive olive-colored shell and synchronized mass nesting
occasions called arribadas. Threats they encounter include habitat deterioration and unintentional catch with fishing gear.
They are found in Sri Lanka and other countries. To preserve the continued existence of these
amazing birds for future generations, conservation efforts concentrate on protecting nesting locations, encouraging ethical
fishing methods, and increasing awareness.




Hawksbill Turtle

Description

The gorgeous hawksbill turtle, also known by its scientific name Eretmochelys imbricata, is found throughout
the world's tropical oceans. This critically endangered species contributes significantly to the preservation
of coral reef ecosystems thanks to its characteristic pointed beak, vivid shell, and intricate patterns. To
safeguard these elegant turtles and their delicate ecosystems for future generations, conservation measures
are essential.




Loggerhead Turtle

Description

The amazing marine reptile known as the loggerhead turtle, or Caretta caretta, may be found in oceans all around the world.
This threatened species, known for its enormous head and strong jaws, migrates great distances to build its nests.
To preserve the survival of these majestic birds and the importance of their function in marine
ecosystems, efforts must be made to protect nesting sites and lessen threats like
pollution and fishing bycatch.
